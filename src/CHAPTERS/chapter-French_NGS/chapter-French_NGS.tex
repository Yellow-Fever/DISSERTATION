\chapter{Deep Sequencing of French Neurotropic Yellow Fever
	Vaccine Strains Reveals A Conserved Genotype and Dissimilarity of Population
	Structure}
\section{Abstract}
The live-attenuated \gls{fnv} vaccine strain of \gls{yfv} was derived empirically in 1930 by passage of \gls{wt} \gls{fvv} in mouse brain. The vaccine was administered extensively in French-speaking Africa until discontinuance in 1980, due to high rates of post-vaccination encephalitis in children. The first deep sequencing study of \gls{fnv} was performed to explore competing hypotheses previously reported to explain the safety profile of the vaccine, using four vaccine strains from different institutional collections. Diversity and specific population structures were compared in reference to the \gls{wt} parental \gls{fvv} strain, and between the vaccines themselves. Lower abundance of polymorphism content was observed for \gls{fnv} strains to each other relative to the parental strain. Although the vaccines were of lower diversity than \gls{fvv}, heterogeneity between the vaccines was observed. Presence of \gls{wt} identity was variable and included revertants to \gls{wt} identity, which was inversely associated with neurovirulence phenotypes previously described \emph{in vivo} for the vaccine strains. The analysis is the first by deep sequencing of an adverse-reactive vaccine, providing novel sequence information and evidence that adaptation of \gls{fnv} to mouse brain was a contributor to the neurotropic adverse phenotype.
\section{Introduction}
Phenotypic adaptation of viruses arising from serial culture and animals is ubiquitous, and constitutes the fundamental property of empirical derivation of live-attenuated vaccines. Of particular significance to the study of live-attenuated vaccines are those administered to prevent infection by \gls{yfv}, which are among the oldest live-attenuated vaccines. \Gls{yfv} was a considerable obstruction to economic development until the empiric derivation of two live-attenuated vaccine strains in the early twentieth century. The vaccines arose from separately isolated parental strains, and distinct attenuating subculture methods. The vaccine strain 17D was developed by passage of \gls{wt} strain Asibi in mouse and chicken tissue in 1936 by the Rockefeller Foundation (New York, USA); derivatives of this strain remain in production today and are administered by the subcutaneous route \parencite{Theiler:1937wy}. 

Concurrent efforts by the Institut Pasteur (Dakar, Senegal) produced the \gls{fnv}, which was administered extensively in French-speaking Africa starting in 1940, more than ten years after the initial adaptation of the virus. \Gls{fnv} was attenuated from the \gls{wt} \gls{fvv}, isolated in 1927 from the Lebanese patient Françoise Mayali, by 128 passages in mouse brain, yielding a strain that was attenuated for both viscerotropism and vector competence; however, neurotropic properties were enhanced \parencite{Lloyd:1933wv,Davis:1932vb, Durieux:1956wx}. \Gls{fnv} was administered by scarification from a reconstitution of infected and desiccated mouse brain, with desirable properties of thermostability and high immunogenicity \parencite{Stuart:1953uaa,Durieux:1956wx}. Commercial lots were administered between passages 250 through 260, relative to the parental strain \parencite{Durieux:1956wx}. Deployment of the \gls{fnv} is credited as a key intervention in the suppression of \gls{yf} disease in French West Africa between 1940 and 1954, with over 84 million doses produced during that time \parencite{Durieux:1956tq,Durieux:1956wx}. \Gls{fnv} was noted for unacceptable rates of post-vaccinal encephalitis in children (3:1000), constituting a rationale for restricting administration of the vaccine to those above 10 years of age in 1961, and later discontinuance of the vaccine in 1980 due to the availability of the safer 17D strain (which is routinely given to those aged 9 months and older) \parencite{Vainio:1998wy}.

Mechanisms of attenuation for both \gls{yfv} vaccines are poorly understood, however information on \gls{fnv} is particularly obscure. \Gls{yfv} strains are variably neurotropic when directly introduced to central nervous tissues of mice, a property that is exploited in lethality and serological protection assays \parencite{Theiler:1930gy,Sawyer:1931wu,Fitzgeorge:1980vl,Barrett:1986iw}. Rhesus and cynomolgous macaques are susceptible to neurotropic disease when challenged by the \gls{ic} route with vaccine strains, of which \gls{fnv} is observed to cause death in monkeys within 5 days; \gls{wt} strains however are not neurotropic and cause viscerotropic disease even when directly introduced in to the brain. In contrast to \gls{fnv}, the widely administered 17D strain vaccines are mildly virulent to monkeys when administered \gls{ic}, causing a transient fever, some evidence of lesions in the brain and survivable encephalitis. Currently, loss of neurovirulence for monkeys is a safety criterion for \gls{yfv} vaccine seeds as required by \gls{who} technical specifications; 17D strain derivatives are the only \gls{yfv} vaccine lineage in current production \parencite{WorldHealthOrganization:2010wo}. 

Two alternative hypotheses have been reported to explain the neurotropic adverse phenotype of \gls{fnv}. First, it is possible that the attenuating passage of \gls{fvv} in mouse brain selected for enhanced capacity of the virus to invade or to productively infect the human central nervous system. \Gls{fnv} was reported to be especially susceptible to binding inhibition by treatment with monkey brain homogenates when compared to other prototypical \gls{yfv} strains \gls{fvv} (\gls{wt} parent of \gls{fnv}), Asibi (\gls{wt} parent of 17D vaccine), and 17D-204 (derived by passage of Asibi strain without nervous tissue) \parencite{Ni:2000un}. Second, it is possible that \gls{fnv} is genetically unstable in the host, leading to \gls{wt} reversion events. Instability of the \gls{fnv} genome was reported after a short cell culture passage series, with concomitant alterations to mouse neurotropism, appearance of putative \gls{wt} genotypes, and greater clonal sequence variation than a 17D strain vaccine strain used as comparator \parencite{Holbrook:2000ut}. \gls{fnv} genome instability under laboratory conditions is unique amongst studied \gls{yfv} strains. \Gls{wt} \gls{yfv} sequences were compared to those of dengue virus by phylogenetic methods, observing relatively low mutation rates for \gls{yfv} \parencite{Sall:2010db}. The 17D vaccine strain was reported by multiple studies to incorporate very few mutations over time under cell culture passage, using Sanger methods \parencite{Xie:1998wi, Pugachev:2003fj}. 

Investigation of the \gls{fnv} adverse phenotype is frustrated by the rarity of reference strains that exist in very few institutional collections; passage histories are not generally accessible for any collection strain. Only one example of \gls{fnv} has been fully sequenced in reference to the \gls{wt} parent \gls{fvv}, and 77 nucleotide changes were observed, with 33 encoding amino acid substitutions \parencite{Wang:1995vu}. Although this study reported one full sequence of the vaccine, neurovirulence was compared between four strains of \gls{fnv} obtained from institutional collections; the vaccines were of unequal neurovirulence in both mice and monkeys [Table \ref{FNV_origins}] \parencite{Wang:1995vu}. Specifically, the four examples of \gls{fnv} were obtained from collections at Porton Down, UK (\gls{fnv}-NT), Yale University (\gls{fnv}-Yale), Institut Pasteur, Paris, France (\gls{fnv}-IP) and \gls{cdc}, Fort Collins, CO (\gls{fnv}-FC). \gls{fnv}-IP and \gls{fnv}-FC were lethal for cynomolgus monkeys following \gls{ic} inoculation while \gls{fnv}-NT was not; \gls{fnv}-Yale was not tested. \Gls{fnv}-NT, \gls{fnv}-Yale and \gls{fnv}-IP were all lethal following \gls{in} inoculation of weanling mice and had short \glspl{ast} (< 6 days) following IC inoculation of weanling mice while \gls{fnv}-FC was not lethal by the \gls{in} route and had a long \gls{ast} following \gls{ic} inoculation (> 11 days). Although it cannot be excluded that the differences in neurovirulence between the strains may have been the result of passaging in individual laboratories, these rare strains of \gls{fnv} represent a context by which to investigate continuous divergence of the central adverse-reactive phenotype that led to discontinuance of the vaccine. 

The reconstruction of population structure for live-attenuated vaccines offers considerable opportunity to exploit viral diversity, an element of quasispecies theory, as a means to understand attenuation and relative stability of viral phenotypes that arose by empiric means. To this end, a deep sequencing approach was previously used to analyze population structure of the commercial \gls{yfv} vaccine strain 17D-204, showing that the vaccine was of lower diversity than the \gls{wt} parental strain Asibi (Beck et al., 2014). From these results, it was postulated that low diversity may contribute to the considerable safety record of 17D vaccine derivatives. For \gls{fnv} reference strains considered in the study, this pattern is recapitulated \emph{in silico} in reference to the 17D strain that is highly stable. Heterogeneity was however observed between \gls{fnv} strains themselves. Furthermore, increase of heterogeneity was associated with previously described loss of neurovirulence \emph{in vivo} for the strains considered. The findings demonstrate that adverse reactions to \gls{fnv} were likely caused by the attenuating passage in nervous tissue, as opposed to reversion in the host.

\subsubsection{In Reference to Previously Sequenced FVV and FNV-IP Strains}
The sequence of \gls{fnv}-IP was previously reported by Wang et al., and compared to \gls{fvv}; 75 nucleotide differences were reported, of which 33 coded for nonsynonymous amino acid substitutions \parencite{Wang:1995vu}. By contrast, the comparison of \gls{fvv} and \gls{fnv}-IP strains in this study revealed 68 nucleotide differences, of which 27 coded for amino acid substitutions. Six coding substitutions reported by Wang et. al. (\gls{e}-Q142R, \gls{e}-G227E, \gls{ns}1-P228L, \gls{ns}3-S25Y, \gls{ns}4B-E246K) were not observed in in the current \gls{fnv}-IP sequence.

It was hypothesized that the balance of these differences between the earlier \gls{fnv}-IP genotype and the sequence generated in this study were the result of differences in the sequence of the parental strains analyzed. A comparison of the previously reported \gls{fvv} (Genbank accession number YFU21056) sequence to that of the newly sequenced \gls{fvv} strain revealed 17 nucleotide differences, of which 7 (\emph{in silico} \gls{fvv} genotype in bold: \gls{e}-\textbf{Q}142R, \gls{e}-\textbf{K}200T, \gls{e}-\textbf{G}227E, \gls{e}-\textbf{R}331K, \gls{ns}1-\textbf{P}228L, \gls{ns}3-\textbf{S}25Y, \gls{ns}4B-\textbf{E}246K) coded for nonsynonymous amino acid substitutions. Thus, differences in the current and previously reported \gls{fnv}-IP genotypes are attributed to differences in the parental reference sequence used.

\section{Discussion}
\subsection{Complex Populations in FNV Are Consistent With Observations Using Other YFV Strains}
Population diversity of \gls{yfv} vaccines, though presently conceived by the quasispecies paradigm, was originally hypothesized in 1935 to explain the efficient adaptation of \gls{fnv} to hepatic passage in rhesus macaques, which selected for \gls{wt} viscerotropism \parencite{FINDLAY:1935vu, Vignuzzi:2006ju}. Conversely, monkey neurotropism is efficiently rescued by passage of the 17D vaccine strain in mouse brain, showing increased morbidity after a single passage cycle \parencite{Meers:1959ui}. Limited diversity is observed in commercial 17D vaccines as evidenced by rare neurotropic variants, which are recoverable at very low frequency from the 17D vaccine strain by plaque purification, and antigenic variants by monoclonal immunoassay \parencite{Liprandi:1981ur,Gould:1989vu}. These studies and others suggest a paradigm by which adverse variability in empirically derived \gls{yfv} vaccines would be enhanced by a poorly controlled adaptive process. 

\subsection{Divergence of FNV Strains in Negative Association With Neurovirulent Phenotype}
\Gls{fnv} lots were produced from seeds of restricted passage level, controlling for sterility, potency, and immunogenicity in monkeys (Durieux, 1956b). In this study \gls{fnv} population structure was examined from four different sources (\gls{fnv}-IP, \gls{fnv}-Yale, \gls{fnv}-FC and \gls{fnv}-NT), plus an example of the originating \gls{fnv} “Dakar” strain of the vaccine was only sequenced at the consensus level due to quality of the sample. Relative to \gls{fvv}, conservation of 26 amino acid substitutions was observed for the four \gls{fnv} examples sequenced [C (2), \gls{pm} (1), \gls{m} (1), \gls{e} (7), \gls{ns}2A (2), \gls{ns}2B (1), \gls{ns}3 (2), \gls{ns}4A (2), \gls{ns}4B (4) and \gls{ns}5 (4)], suggesting that these are the particular amino acids involved in the derivation of \gls{fnv} from \gls{fvv}; however, we cannot exclude that other mutations in the original \gls{fnv} have been lost on passage of the four \gls{fnv} studied here. In particular, six amino acid substitutions (\gls{e}-T7A, \gls{e}-N135K, \gls{e}-M457I, \gls{ns}3-I13V, \gls{ns}4B-A165V, \gls{ns}5-R2T) were conserved between \gls{fnv}-IP, \gls{fnv}-Yale, and \gls{fnv}-FC, but are not observed in \gls{fnv}-NT [Table \ref{subs_main_FNV}]. Thus, it is likely that the attenuation of \gls{fvv} to produce \gls{fnv} involved 26 amino acid substitutions. 

It is reasonably hypothesized from these data that the low abundance of variant amino acid substitutions in \gls{fnv}-Yale, both by consensus sequence and by variant reconstruction, reflects a short and protected passage history from the original vaccine, as \gls{fnv}-Yale has been maintained in a low passage virus collection (\gls{wrceva}). A comparison of \gls{fnv} strains in mice showed that \gls{fnv}-Yale produced the shortest \gls{ast} following both \gls{ic} and \gls{in} inoculation compared to \gls{fnv}-IP and \gls{fnv}-NT. [Table \ref{FNV_origins}] \parencite{Wang:1995vu}. Likewise the \gls{fnv}-NT strain, observed in this study to be the most divergent of \gls{yfv} vaccines, was previously recognized by Wang et al to be nonlethal in cynomolgus monkeys (\gls{ast} > 30 days) whereas \gls{fnv}-IP and \gls{fnv}-FC were lethal; \gls{fnv}-Yale was not tested \parencite{Wang:1995vu}. Therefore it is likely that \gls{fnv}-IP and \gls{fnv}-Yale are of least divergence from \gls{fnv} production lots, representing a genotype with high relative fixation to nervous tissue; this genotype would likely confer the greatest adverse neurotropic properties in humans.

Two distinct patterns arise in the diversity profiles of the vaccines when only the 26 amino acid substitutions between \gls{fvv} and \gls{fnv} are considered [Table \ref{FNV_entropy}]. First, a set of positions were characterized by low diversity in \gls{fnv}-IP and \gls{fnv}-Yale that is progressively greater in \gls{fnv}-FC and then greatest for \gls{fnv}-NT (example: M-L36F, \gls{fvv}: 0.182, \gls{fnv}-IP: 0.014, \gls{fnv}-Yale: 0.004, \gls{fnv}-FC: 0.014, \gls{fnv}-NT: 0.038). Broadly, these sites are represented in structural regions of the \gls{fnv} genomes, an expected result in consideration of evidence that envelope gene residues exert particular influence over \gls{yfv} neurovirulence \parencite{Ni:2000un}. Second, a distinct set of positions were also characterized by low diversity in \gls{fnv}-IP and \gls{fnv}-Yale, however the greatest relative entropies are observed for \gls{fnv}-NT, and then second greatest for \gls{fnv}-FC (example: \gls{ns}4B-V98I, \gls{fvv}: 0.128, \gls{fnv}-IP: 0.011, \gls{fnv}-Yale: 0.020, \gls{fnv}-FC: 0.126, \gls{fnv}-NT: 0.033). Because \gls{fnv}-FC was previously reported in mice to be both less neurovirulent and also less mouse neuroinvasive than the other vaccines (by the \gls{in} and \gls{ic} routes), it is possible that the sites with high relative diversity in \gls{fnv}-FC are determinants of mouse neuroinvasion. By \gls{rmsd}, consensus \gls{wt} revertants cluster at limited genetic distance from the parental strain, this is particularly evident for the \gls{fnv}-NT strain [Figure \ref{Scatter_FNV_RMSD}]. This finding suggests that particular residues in the vaccine are selected to revert to \gls{wt} under laboratory conditions.

\subsection{Inference of a Compact Genotype Underlying Neurotropism of FNV}
Using the consensus data in concert with sitewise diversity estimations, it is possible to hypothesize a set of residues that contribute to neurovirulence in both monkeys and mice. In particular, the destabilization of entropy for 9 amino acid substitutions in the M [2] , E [4], \gls{ns}4B [2] and \gls{ns}5 [1] proteins (\gls{pm}-V25M, \gls{m}-L36F, \gls{e}-T7A, \gls{e}-A54V, \gls{e}-N153K, \gls{e}-N249D, \gls{ns}4B-L19S, \gls{ns}4B-A165V, and \gls{ns}5-R2T in \gls{fnv}-NT indicates that this smaller set of residues contributes to the attenuated phenotype in monkeys. Conversely, the destabilization of five amino acid substitutions in \gls{ns}3 [1], \gls{ns}4B [1] and \gls{ns}5 [3] (\gls{ns}3-I13V, \gls{ns}4B-V98I, \gls{ns}5-I285T, \gls{ns}5-R335K, and \gls{ns}5-F531L) in \gls{fnv}-FC indicates that this smaller set of residues contributes to the neuroinvasive and neurovirulent phenotypes in mice. 

\subsection{Amino Acid Substitutions are Similar Between FNV and 17D, Although Attenuated By Different Passage Tissues}
Notably, the substitutions \gls{m}-L36F, \gls{e}-K200T, \gls{e}-K331R, and \gls{ns}4B-I95M were observed in common for the attenuation processes between \gls{fvv} to \gls{fnv}, and Asibi to 17D [Table \ref{subs_main_FNV}]. Flavivirus \gls{m} protein lies contains a pro-apoptotic domain that, upon transfection and overexpression, is attenuated in phenotype when phenylalanine is substituted at residue 36 \parencite{Catteau:2003dl}. Because the substitution at \gls{e}-K200T is shared by Asibi and all \gls{fnv} strains, it is likely either a cell culture adaptation or compensatory, and not consequential to the attenuated phenotype. Despite being associated with mouse neuroinvasion the residue did not singularly affect the phenotype of a cloned 17D virus (Lee and Lobigs, 2008). The residue E-K331R was associated with both attenuation of Asibi to yield 17D and experimental adaptation of the Asibi strain to hamster liver \parencite{McArthur:2003fv}, so the contribution to vaccine attenuation is complicated by the paucity of animal models and the difficulty to parse neurotropic from viscerotropic determinants of pathogenicity. The function of \gls{ns}4B mutations in attenuation of the vaccine is not understood, however evidence has been put forth to suggest roles for \gls{ns}4B in both interferon antagonism and replication complex associations \parencite{Zou:2014ku,MunozJordan:2005ia}. 

\subsection{Reversion to Wild-Type is Associated with Loss of Neurotropism for FNV}
Reversion to \gls{wt} is observed for \gls{fnv} strains not only at the consensus level, but also for small population variants. In terms of both raw variant counts and revertants, the strains varied by virtue of hypothesized divergence from the original vaccine, e.g. \gls{fnv}-IP and \gls{fnv}-Yale alignments, revealed the relatively low counts of both variants and revertants [Table \ref{FNV_revertants}]. Conversely, counts of variants and revertants were higher in \gls{fnv}-FC and \gls{fnv}-NT, which are hypothesized to be divergent, presumably due to the passage history of these viruses in their institutions. Though genome-scale estimations of diversity are similar between all \gls{fnv} strains and 17D-204; as expected, diversity of \gls{fvv} was greater than for the vaccines. The processing and alignment methods used in these analyses were more stringent than previously used for the Asibi strain (Chapter 3), reducing observable differences in estimated diversity between Asibi and 17D-204 \parencite{Beck:2014cm}. However, density comparison shows that 17D-204 variants are fixed to lower aggregate frequencies than those of Asibi, \gls{fvv}, or the \gls{fnv} vaccines, and also that Asibi contains a small coding and silent variant population at higher frequencies than the other viruses [Figure \ref{FNV_density_all} and Figure \ref{FNV_density_NS}]. This pattern may contribute to the superior safety record of 17D vaccine compared to \gls{fnv}, and supports a hypothesis that diversity influencing the vaccine phenotype arises from a limited number of sites, and that some tolerable level of low-frequency variants are contained in otherwise fixed vaccine preparations.

\subsection{Parental Strains FVV and Asibi Are Less Divergent than Previously Described and Likely Originated from the Same Epidemic Event}
An alignment of current reference consensus sequences for Asibi (Genbank accession AY640589.1) and \gls{fvv} (Genbank accession YFU21056.1) shows 21 nucleotide differences, of which 8 are coding changes (not shown) \parencite{Wang:1995vu}. The current study indicates lesser divergence of the parental strains than was previously described by Sanger methods undertaken 20 years ago, revealing instead 11 consensus nucleotide differences and two amino acid substitutions in this study. \gls{fvv} and Asibi were both isolated in 1927 at considerable geographic separation (Senegal and Ghana, respectively); historical and research literature indicates that the epidemic was far-reaching in West Africa and encompassed both territories where the parental strains were collected \parencites{Stokes:1928uh}[415]{Bazin:2011vi}. The limited divergence observed in this study by \gls{ngs} methods supports a conclusion that both parental strains originated from the same \gls{yf} epidemic event or reservoir population. This finding is consistent with previous observations of the low mutation rates of \gls{yfv} strains in nature, suggesting that Asibi and \gls{fvv} would be genetically similar if concurrently sampled, even at distance \parencite{Sall:2010db}. 	

\section{Conclusions}
A hypothesis of low diversity for the live-attenuated vaccine strains compared to\gls{wt} parental viruses was confirmed in these data. Because the reconstruction of highly diverse virus populations are ultimately a proxy for the inspection of low-abundance genotypes, strategies of this type can be advanced in a predictive context. Significantly, viral population structures have been reconstructed for \gls{fnv} that reveal divergence of an empiric \gls{fnv} vaccine from a putative original, adapted genotype. In this context, the extent of adaptation and fixation to substrate tissue by a vaccine strain was measured, revealing instability in \gls{fnv} strain population structures. Reasonably, these features demonstrate an essential dependence upon culture conditions for the fixed genotype to be retained in the vaccine. The presented data support previous findings that employed classical techniques, including evidence of instability for the \gls{fnv} neurotropic phenotype \emph{in vivo} \parencite{Holbrook:2000ut,Wang:1995vu}. The findings provide further evidence that viral population structure should be considered in safety assessments of live-attenuated vaccine technologies.
